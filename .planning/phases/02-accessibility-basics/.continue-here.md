<context>
The same-device A/B probe experiments established the MIUI-stable accessibility profile:
- `accessibilityEventTypes="typeAllMask"` is required
- `FLAG_INCLUDE_NOT_IMPORTANT_VIEWS` is required
- `canTakeScreenshot` is not required for bind stability

Those findings were applied back to the production `GhostAccessibilityService`, after which `/tree`, `/find`, and `/tap` succeeded end-to-end on the target MIUI phone and P2 Task 4 was closed. P3 work then added `/swipe`, a dedicated swipe probe, and truthful scroll-position diagnostics. Manual shell swipe on the real probe changes the probe signal, but Ghosthand accessibility `/swipe` can still fail when the framework accessibility connection is absent. A focused instrumentation pass showed `/swipe` may attempt to dispatch on the correct service instance while the underlying framework connection is gone, so a bounded readiness fix was added to expose `dispatchCapable` and fail honestly with `ACCESSIBILITY_UNAVAILABLE`.
</context>
